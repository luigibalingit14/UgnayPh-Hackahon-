## Competition Guidelines

### GENERAL GUIDELINES 
1. This competition is open to all high schools, colleges, and universities nationwide. However, students from BatStateU are not allowed to join, with the exception of students from BatStateU Integrated School. 
2. Teams may consist of three to five members students currently enrolled in the current semester. 
3. Members may come from different academic institutions. 
4. For teams with members from the same institution, multiple participating teams are allowed. 
5. If the team is composed of students from different programs/strands, the team leader must be pursuing the following programs/strands: 
   - Bachelor of Science in Computer Science 
   - Bachelor of Science in Information Technology 
   - Bachelor of Science in Data Science 
   - Bachelor of Science in Information Systems 
   - or any other software-related degree program or tech-related senior high school strand 
6. Team members may come from any academic discipline to encourage multidisciplinary collaboration, but teams consisting of those pursuing the same program/strand is also allowed. 
7. Registration will be open until March 8, 2026 (Thursday), 5:00 PM. 
8. Attendance at the seminar and orientation to be held on March 16, 2026 is required. 
9. The hackathon will focus on digital solutions. The specific challenges will be released on the day of the orientation. 
10. The preliminary round will require a video to be submitted on or before March 22, 2026 (11:59 PM). 
    - The video must demonstrate the prototype, highlighting its features and functionality.  
    - It should explain the problem being addressed, the proposed solution, and its intended real-world application.  
    - Teams must clearly state the technologies used in the project, including programming languages, frameworks, libraries, platforms, APIs, and AI tools, if any.  
    - The video should accurately represent the actual state of the prototype, and purely conceptual or slide-only presentations without a functional demonstration may receive lower evaluations. 
    - The video may range from three minutes to five minutes. Failure to comply with the time requirement may result in deductions. 
11. The source code must be submitted via a link to a public GitHub repository. While a README file is not required, including one is highly encouraged, as it can help judges better understand your project. 
12. The entries will be posted on the Facebook page of CICS - SC Alangilan on March 25, 2026 (6:00 PM), and reactions will be counted for the People’s Choice Award until April 1, 2026 (6:00 PM). 
13. Finalists will be announced on March 30, 2026 and will have a preparation period for their pitch deck from March 31, 2026 to April 9, 2026. 
14. The finalists will pitch their idea synchronously via Zoom on April 10, 2026. The pitch deck will be submitted on or before April 9, 2026 (11:59 PM). 
15. For further inquiries, you may contact the following: 
    - VINCE CLYDE S. BANAAG (Event Head)
    - 22-06373@g.batstate-u.edu.ph 

### RULES AND RESTRICTIONS 
1. Only registered participants are allowed to compete and submit outputs. 
2. Projects must not promote harm, discrimination, misinformation, or illegal activities.  
3. Participants are required to fully disclose the use of any AI-powered tools, platforms, or services utilized during the development of their project. 
4. Any programming language, framework, or platform may be used unless explicitly restricted. 
5. Participants must observe academic honesty, professionalism, and respectful behavior. 
6. Failure to comply with the rules, deadlines, or disclosure requirements may lead to penalties or disqualification.

### Criteria for Assessment 
- **30% - Mastery and Use of Software Concepts:** Evaluates how effectively the team applies relevant concepts, techniques, and technologies to develop a functional and well-designed solution. Emphasis is placed on overall quality, efficiency, and appropriate use of available tools and resources. 
- **30% - Novelty and Innovation:** Assesses the originality of the project and the creativity behind its concept and implementation. This includes how the solution introduces new ideas, improves existing approaches, or applies technology in a unique and meaningful way. 
- **30% - Real-world Impact and Viability:** Measures how relevant the project is to real-world problems and its potential for practical deployment. Consideration is given to feasibility, scalability, sustainability, and the overall benefit to users or communities. 
- **10% - Compliance to Rules and Restrictions:** Determines the extent to which the project follows all competition guidelines, technical constraints, ethical standards, and submission requirements. Failure to comply may result in point deductions or disqualification.
